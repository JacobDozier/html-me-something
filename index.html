We've been to Mexico, twice!

My wife and I decided that we wanted to leave the country for our honeymoon. We saved up for a long time, on top of the already expensive wedding planning. This is the story, highlights really, of our honeymoon and why we loved it so much that we decided to go back.

Crown Paradise Golden, Puerto Vallarta, Jalisco, MEX

Puerto Vallarta is a tourist city nestled next to Bahia de Banderas and this was among the first things we saw. The resort was on of the older ones in the city, being the second in the row once in the city proper. It was like something out of a movie. We set our bags down to sign in and we turned around to a bell boy holding out fruity drinks for us. When we took the drinks, he took our bags and off we went.

Cookin Vallarta

You know how they say, "Save the best for last"? We didn't do that on this trip. We decided to take a cooking class while in Mexico, so that we would have the ability to recreate at least some part of our trip. This cooking class was easily the best part of the trip. Chef Enrique brought us to the local market where we picked fresh ingredients and sampled hand made tamales. Then, he brought us back to his home to cook with his wife and sister. We cooked, we laughed, and we drank tequila. Best food I've ever had, with some of the nicest people I've ever met. If you ever go to Puerto Vallarta and want a cultural experience: take the Cookin Vallarta food tour with Chef Enrique, seriously.

Lazy by the Sea

This was the first moment in during our honeymoon where we got to just sit back and take in the fact that we were husband and wife. We spent the rest of this day playing in the ocean, eating, drinking, and sleeping. Not much to say here other than this was Brittany's first, and my second, time swimming in the Ocean. Although, it was a first for both of us to swim in the Pacific Ocean.

Beach Party

Perhaps "Beach Party" is a bit of an exageration, but as you can see we had fun. Funny story, haggling is alive and well in Mexico. Lico, the guy in the white shirt, noticed us walking on the beach and asked us if we wanted to go parasailing. I replied with, "Who wouldn't," and he brought us to his umbrella to negotiate. He proposed a price per person and I turned to Brittany to discuss it. I didn't want to spend all of the dollars we had left, we exchanged most of our cash for pesos. Lico heard this and said to forget the price. For the same price, he offered the parasailing and a ziplining tour in the Sierra Madres to the southeast of Puerto Vallarta. The funny part was that I wasn't even trying to haggle.

Room With A View

After a long day of playing on the beach we wanted to relax. There was a problem however, our room's air conditioner was broken. I had gotten to 32 degrees Celcius in our room and we were not having it. The maintenance tech trying to fix it learned that we were on our honeymoon and exlaimed, "You are on your honeymoon?!? Why aren't you in a suite?!?" We explained that we asked if we could upgrade but that the concierge said that they were all full. This was apparently not the case as the maintenance tech could not fix the room's A/C and we were upgraded to a suite for free. We saw him as we were "carrying" our bags to the new room and he quietly exlaimed, "I did it!!!" as we walked by, beaming ear to ear.

Leaving

This was the saddest part of the trip, leaving. We were honestly hoping that the hurricane, a hundred miles east, would ground all of the planes and we would be forced to stay.

Round Two!

Aaaaannnnddd were back! We were so happy to be back in the city where we celebrated our nuptials. We were in a different, better in our opinion, resort. The views from these resorts are truely beautiful and our shoddy selfie does not due it justice.

Scale in Beauty

After settling in our room and eating we walked the beach reminiscing about our last visit. I decided to take a picture of Bahia de Banderas, which we learned was one of the largest naturally occuring bays in the world. On the right and left you can see the mountains in the distance that enclose the bay. There is also an island in the bay that you cannot see, that is how big it is.

Cabeza de los Muertos

It just so happened that there was a vendor on the grounds of the resort that offered clay skulls that you could paint. Everyone has heard of Dia de los Muertos, "The Day of the Dead," and it is very important in Mexico. The vendor adds some more traditional decorations and takes it for the night to be fired in a kiln. You come back the next day and you have a beautiful souvenir that has significant culture meaning. It also doubles as an awesome halloween decoration (Dia de los Muertos != Halloween).

Corona Factory

We were en route to a tour of the city and the guide pointed out the Corona factory to our left. He jokingly said that there is a secret pipe under the road from the factory to the military base. Everyone on the bus had a good laugh.
